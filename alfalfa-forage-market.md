<p><p>The alfalfa forage market consists of two main types: dried pulp and wet pulp. Dried pulp is produced by dehydrating alfalfa, resulting in a nutrient-dense product with long shelf life, ideal for livestock feed and exporting due to its lightweight nature. Conversely, wet pulp retains moisture, providing a fresher option that can be beneficial for immediate consumption by animals but has a shorter shelf life. Both types cater to different production needs and preferences in the agricultural sector.</p></p>
