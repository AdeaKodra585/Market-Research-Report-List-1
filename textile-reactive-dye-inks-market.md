<p><p>The textile reactive dye inks market is divided into two main types: digital printing and traditional printing. Digital printing uses inkjet technology to apply dyes directly onto fabric, allowing for intricate designs and quick production times. In contrast, traditional printing methods, such as screen or roller printing, involve applying dyes through stencils or rollers, often suited for large-scale runs. Both methods utilize reactive dyes, which chemically bond with fibers, offering vibrant colors and excellent wash fastness in textile applications.</p></p>
